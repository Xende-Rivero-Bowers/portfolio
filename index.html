About me

My name is Xende, and I'm a fourth-year student studying Maths and Computer Science at Durham University. My main interest is video game design and testing. At the moment I am working for the University as a Computer Science demonstrator and a marker for Mathematics undergraduates, as well as volunteering at my college library. I also enjoy playing the classical guitar, and have achieved the DipLCM in classical guitar performance. My main hobby is reading, and my favourite author is Terry Pratchett, particularly his series The Science of Discworld.

Other coding work

Here are some samples of other non-game-related coursework I did for my degree. While they are not related to game development, they show my understanding of C++, HTML and Python. I received Firsts for all my coursework, and was awarded the Outstanding Achievement in Natural Sciences award for achieving exceptionally high marks in each of them.

Connect 4 coursework (Download)

This coursework was to design a game of Connect 4 in C++, with the twist that the game is played on a cylinder.

Image Processing coursework (Download)

This coursework was to use Python to design four functions that could be used on images: A light effect, a pencil sketch filter, a smoothing filter and a swirl effect.

Data Collection Coursework (Download)

This coursework was to use Python and HTML to search for appearances of certain words on the BBC website, then find out how strongly correlated each word is to each other and plot the results on a heat map.
